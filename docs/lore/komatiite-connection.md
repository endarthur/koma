# The Komatiite Connection: Ultramafic = Ultra-Basic

## What is a Komatiite?

**Komatiite** is an ultramafic volcanic rock - one of the most magnesium-rich, silicon-poor volcanic rocks known. It represents some of Earth's oldest volcanic activity, erupted from a hotter mantle 2.7-3.5 billion years ago.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Ultramafic composition:** >18% MgO, <45% SiO2
- **Ultra-basic chemistry:** The most basic (least acidic) volcanic rock
- **High temperature:** Erupted at 1600-1650°C (modern basalts: ~1200°C)
- **Ancient:** Primarily Archean age (>2.5 billion years old)
- **Primitive:** Close to mantle composition, minimal differentiation

## The Geological Parallel

Dr. Margaret Keleman explained it in a 1986 interview:

> "When you look at a komatiite in thin section, you're looking at something incredibly pure and simple. It's not a complex granite with twelve different minerals and a complicated history. It's olivine, maybe some pyroxene, that's it. It's as close as you get to pure mantle material reaching the surface."
>
> "That's what we wanted for computing. Not another complex operating system with layers upon layers of abstraction. Something close to the metal, direct, powerful. Ultra-basic doesn't mean simple-minded. It means fundamental. Essential. Pure."

## Why Ultramafic?

The chemistry analogy works on multiple levels:

### 1. Basic vs. Acidic (pH Analogy)

In geology, **basic** rocks have low silica (SiO2), **acidic** rocks have high silica:
- Acidic: Rhyolite (>69% SiO2) - complex, evolved, differentiated
- Intermediate: Andesite (~57-63% SiO2)
- Basic: Basalt (~45-52% SiO2)
- Ultrabasic: Komatiite (>18% MgO, <45% SiO2) - primitive, simple, fundamental

### 2. Mantle Connection

Komatiites come *directly from the mantle*. They haven't been:
- Differentiated by fractional crystallization
- Contaminated by crustal material
- Modified by complex processes

**Koma is the same:** Direct access to the kernel (Olivine). No unnecessary abstraction layers. From mantle to terminal, from kernel to shell.

### 3. Temperature = Power

Komatiites erupted at incredibly high temperatures. That heat represents energy, power, capability.

**Koma's power:** Not from complexity, but from direct access. Hot, powerful, fundamental.

### 4. Ancient = Timeless

Komatiites are Archean - some of Earth's oldest rocks. But their simplicity makes them *timeless*. We still study them. They still teach us about planetary processes.

**Koma's design:** Not trendy, not fashionable. Timeless Unix principles. Will be relevant in 40 years.

## Field Locations

The name carries geological pilgrimage sites:

### Komati River, South Africa
Where komatiites were first named (1969). The type locality. Every geologist who works on komatiites eventually visits the Komati River in the Barberton Greenstone Belt.

### Abitibi Greenstone Belt, Canada
World-class komatiite sequences. Where Craton Systems did early field testing of the Koma Field Terminal.

### Yilgarn Craton, Western Australia
Some of the best-preserved komatiites on Earth. Where spinifex textures were first properly described.

### Greenland Archean Craton
Ancient komatiites in some of Earth's oldest rocks. Where Koma Workstations processed field data in real-time for the first time.

## The Color Palette Connection

The Koma Workstation's colors come directly from komatiite geology:

### Phosphor Green (#00ff88) = Olivine
The primary mineral in komatiites. Fresh olivine is a beautiful pale green. When you shine light through thin sections of komatiite, olivine crystals glow green.

The terminal's phosphor green isn't just retro aesthetics - it's olivine under the microscope.

### Lava Orange (#ff6b35) = Hot Komatiite Lava
Komatiites erupted at 1600-1650°C - hot enough to glow orange-red. The hottest lavas on modern Earth.

The accent color represents that thermal power, that energy, that primitive heat from Earth's early mantle.

### Deep Charcoal (#1a1a1a) = Weathered Basalt
The dark background. Aged volcanic rock, stable foundation, the crust we stand on.

## The Science

**Komatiite composition (typical):**
```
SiO2:  44-48%
MgO:   18-32%
FeO:   8-11%
Al2O3: 5-10%
CaO:   5-9%
```

**Mineralogy:**
- Olivine (primary)
- Pyroxene (less common)
- Chromite (accessory)
- Serpentine (alteration)

**Texture:**
- Spinifex (acicular olivine crystals)
- Cumulate (settled olivine)
- Massive (fine-grained)

**Age:**
- Primarily 2.7-3.5 Ga (Archean)
- Rare Proterozoic examples
- No modern equivalents
